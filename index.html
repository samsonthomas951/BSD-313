Biography

Andrej Karpathy is a well-known Slovak-Canadian computer scientist. He was born on Oct. 23, 1986, in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia (now Slovakia) and immigrated to Toronto, Canada, at 15. He graduated from the University of Toronto with a Bachelor's in Computer Science and Physics in 2009 and later attended the University of British Columbia for his Master's in 2011, focusing on machine learning for agile robotics. Karpathy earned his PhD. from Stanford University, working with Fei-Fei Li, on the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing.

Career

Karpathy became one of the original staff members at OpenAI in 2015 when he joined as a research scientist. He works at Tesla, where he focuses on artificial intelligence and leads a computer vision group for the company's Autopilot system. In 2023, he returned to OpenAI after a sabbatical to help enhance GPT-4 for ChatGPT. In July 2024, he launched Eureka Labs, which is an education platform integrated with AI to transform the way we learn through artificial intelligence.

Education

Karpathy has focused on AI education in his career. He developed and taught one of the most popular classes at Stanford: CS 231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition, Stanford’s first deep learning course. His teaching videos on neural networks have received wide acclaim testament to his passion for making complex AI concepts digestible.
